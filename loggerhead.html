About Loggerhead Sea Turtles

Loggerhead sea turtles are large marine reptiles that can be found in oceans around the world. They are known for their large heads and powerful jaws. Loggerheads are named after their exceptionally large heads, which support powerful jaw muscles.

Habitat

Loggerhead sea turtles are commonly found in temperate and tropical waters. They prefer coastal habitats and can often be seen near coral reefs and seagrass beds. These turtles undertake long migrations, traveling thousands of miles each year.

Conservation

Loggerhead sea turtles are listed as a threatened species. They face numerous threats, including habitat destruction, pollution, fishing nets, and climate change. Conservation efforts aim to protect nesting beaches, reduce fishing bycatch, and raise awareness about the importance of preserving these magnificent creatures.
