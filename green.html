About Green Sea Turtles

Globally, tropical and subtropical oceans are home to the enormous marine reptile known as the green sea turtle. They're renowned for their vivid green hue and their propensity for traveling great miles in search of food and a place to lay their eggs.

Habitat

Coral reefs, seagrass meadows, and shallow coastal waters are among the coastal habitats used by green sea turtles. For eating and breeding, they are dependent on certain areas. These herbivorous turtles eat seagrass and algae as their main sources of food.

Conservation

The lack of habitat, pollution, and illegal hunting are just a few of the concerns that have led to the green sea turtle's listing as an endangered species. The main goals of conservation efforts are to conserve nesting beaches, enforce fishing rules, and increase public understanding of the need of saving these majestic animals.
