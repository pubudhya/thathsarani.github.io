Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

About Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery in Sri Lanka is devoted to the preservation and protection of sea turtles. It is essential for protecting the eggs and releasing the hatchlings into the water.

Informations

Conservation Efforts:
The sea turtle hatchery aims to safeguard eggs, young hatchlings, and ill or damaged adult turtles. They remove turtle eggs from shoreline nests that are at risk and place them in hatchery protected locations. The hatchlings' chances of survival are improved as a result.

Species:
The Green turtle, Loggerhead turtle, Olive Ridley turtle, Hawksbill turtle, and Leatherback turtle are the five marine turtle species that may be found in Sri Lanka, and they are the main focus of the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery. All of these species are regarded as severely endangered or endangered.

Turtle Releasing

Visit the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery if you want to learn more about sea turtles and contribute to their protection. You may see the amazing work being done and take part in the turtle release into the ocean.
