About Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtles

Kemp's Ridley sea turtles are the smallest and most endangered sea turtle species. They are known for their olive-gray or greenish-gray shells and triangular-shaped heads. Kemp's Ridleys are primarily found in the Gulf of Mexico and along the Atlantic coast of the United States.

Habitat

Kemp's Ridley sea turtles inhabit warm shallow waters, including coastal bays, estuaries, and nearshore areas. They are known for their mass nesting events called "arribadas," where large numbers of females come ashore to lay their eggs. These turtles primarily feed on crabs, shrimp, and other marine invertebrates.

Conservation

Kemp's Ridley sea turtles are critically endangered due to numerous threats, including habitat destruction, pollution, entanglement in fishing gear, and illegal egg collection. Conservation efforts focus on protecting nesting beaches, implementing turtle-friendly fishing practices, and raising awareness to ensure the survival of this species.
