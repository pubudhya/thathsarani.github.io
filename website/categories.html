TURTLE CATEGORIES

Green Sea Turtle

The Green Sea Turtle is known for its greenish-colored fat and cartilage.

Loggerhead Sea Turtle

The Loggerhead Sea Turtle has a large head and powerful jaws.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

The Hawksbill Sea Turtle has a distinctive hawk-like beak and colorful carapace.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

The Leatherback Sea Turtle is the largest species of sea turtle with a leathery shell.

Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle

Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle is the smallest and most endangered species of sea turtle.

Olive Ridley Sea Turtle

Olive Ridley Sea Turtle has an olive-colored carapace and is known for mass nesting events.

Species Common Name Scientific Name Habitat Size and Weight Diet
Olive Ridley Turtle Olive Ridley Lepidochelys olivacea Warm waters, tropical and subtropical regions, open ocean and coastal areas Up to 65 cm (25.6 in) in length, weighs around 34-45 kg (75-100 lbs) Crustaceans, jellyfish, mollusks, algae, and seagrass
Leatherback Turtle Leatherback Dermochelys coriacea Global distribution, both in tropical and colder waters, pelagic and coastal areas Largest of all sea turtles, can reach up to 180 cm (6 ft) in length, weighs around 250-700 kg (550-1,540 lbs) Predominantly jellyfish, but also eat other soft-bodied organisms
Green Turtle Green Chelonia mydas Tropical and subtropical coastal waters, seagrass beds, coral reefs Up to 120 cm (47 in) in length, weighs around 150-190 kg (330-420 lbs) Seagrass, algae, sea lettuce, and other marine vegetation
Hawksbill Turtle Hawksbill Eretmochelys imbricata Tropical and subtropical regions, coral reefs, rocky coastlines Around 75-90 cm (29-35 in) in length, weighs around 45-70 kg (100-150 lbs) Sponges, algae, jellyfish, sea anemones, and other invertebrates
Loggerhead Turtle Loggerhead Caretta caretta Coastal and open ocean habitats, temperate and tropical waters Around 90-110 cm (35-43 in) in length, weighs around 115-170 kg (250-375 lbs) Crabs, clams, sea urchins, and other hard-shelled organisms
